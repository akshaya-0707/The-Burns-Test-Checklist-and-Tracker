# The-Burns-Test-Checklist-and-Tracker
This tool brings the Burns Depression Checklist into a simple, repeatable weekly ritual. Every Monday, you work through the same set of questions covering mood, motivation, self-esteem, physical symptoms, and other key indicators tied to depressive symptoms. Instead of just answering once and forgetting about it, your responses are saved and scored automatically, so you can watch how your numbers shift from week to week.
The interface is built for quick, low-friction check-ins: a clean checklist, a clear scoring summary, and a visual history view (such as a line chart or simple table) that shows your trend over time rather than just a single snapshot. This makes it easier to notice patterns, like a dip during a stressful month or steady improvement after a change in routine, that might be hard to see day to day.
It's designed as a personal self-awareness tool, not a diagnostic instrument. The Burns checklist itself is a self-report screening tool, and consistent tracking can help you and, if you choose to share it, a therapist or doctor get a clearer picture of how you're doing over time. It works well alongside therapy or medication management, or simply as a way to stay attuned to your own mental health.
